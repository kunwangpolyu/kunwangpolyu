ABOUT

Dr Wang Kun is currently Assistant Professor of the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering, Hong Kong Polytechnic University. Before that, he is a tenured Associate Professor in The University of International Business and Economics (UIBE), Beijing, China. He obtained his BBA (first-class Honor) and MPhil degree from the Hong Kong Polytechnic University, the PhD degree from Sauder School of Business, University of British Columbia (UBC). His thesis has won the Best Graduate Student Paper Award, the US Transportation Research Forum 58th Annual Meeting, Chicago, US.

Dr Wang has the research interests in transport and logistics management, focusing on aviation operations and management, transport environmental issues, intermodal competition and cooperation. He published more than 100 SSCI/SCI journal papers, with nearly 40 papers on top tier transportation journals, like Transportation Research Part A/B/C/D/E, also several papers on renowned economics journals (International Journal of Industrial and Organization, Energy Economics, Journal of Transport Economics and Policy). His work has been widely cited with Google Scholar citations more than 4000 times, and 13 articles have been selected into the ESI highly cited papers. He is awarded the “World's Top 2% Scientists by Stanford University” in 2023 and 2024. Dr Wang also serves as Editor of Transport Policy (SSCI JCRQ1), Associate Editor of Journal of Air Transport Management (SSCI, JCR Q2), and Editorial Board Members for Transportation Research Part A (SSCI, JCRQ1, ABS 3 star), Transportation Research Part D (SSCI, JCRQ1), Research in Transportation Business and Management (RTBM) and several other journals.

As principal investigator (PI), Dr Wang has acquired several external research grants, including 2 NSFC grants (国家自然科学基金), and other grants from Guangdong, Beijing Municipal governments, Ministry of Education of China etc. He has also conducted many consultancy projects for governments and industries, including Civil Aviation Administration of China (CAAC), Airport Authority of Hong Kong (AAHK), Beijing Municipal Government, Xiamen Airlines, Manchester Airport Group. Several policy reports are submitted to governments, including one endorsed by national-level leader (正国级).
